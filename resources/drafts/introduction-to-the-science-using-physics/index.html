I've been pondering how I would teach Physics if I more control of the syllabus and scheduling. The flaws of the current system are numerous and frankly go beyond Physics so let us focus on the few that are within our grasp.

Flaws in the system

0. Prior Art

[1] https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/48WeP7oTec3kBEada/two-more-things-to-unlearn-from-school

Aim for compression of course material so expend time in a more effective manner. In this sense we are saying reduce 'wasted' teaching time to free up class time to actually teach. This would be with the aim of diluting the bullshit so students actually want to engage.

  • Physics courses should teach the scientific method
  • Computer science should be programming
  • Mathematics should be the joy of numbers

1. Guessing the Teachers Password

What does you in is not failure to apply some high-level, intricate, complicated technique. It’s overlooking the basics. Not keeping your eye on the ball. -- Jerry Cleaver

First we must look at the proverbial elephant in the room. Any teacher who has spent any time in a classroom will tell you that children are expected to memorise and repeat content. Students over time are taught that guessing the pattern of answers (or less politely bullshitting) is the key to success.

In Physics teaching we focus heavily on reciting past glories of the subject and ensuring our students learn them. More than that we arbitrarily pick sections of the subject to exemplfy. As a test, ask a child what physics is.

For such a simple question you are unlikely to get a valid answer. I have heard answers ranging from the mechanical (how to describe the movement of stuff) to the inaccurate (study of the universe).

2. Atomiticity

Content is often presented as individual silos of knowledge. This ignores the rich overlap between topics.

3. Regurgitation

4. Patronising Simplicity

5. Failure to refactor

Finally a pet peeve of mine. Physics is littered with inaccurate definitions and antiquated approaches. In the same way that US English destroys the etymological link between (some) words and their past, we teach concepts in Physics without any link to why they are there in the first place!

Do you know what a 'ray' is? A ray is an invisible action at a distance as Physicists of the era were perplexed by a whole host of phenomena, that could not be explained using purely classical physics. And whilst that is interesting, it is not physics.

A student does not need to know what Cathode Rays are. Nor should we still be teaching the antiquated nonsense of alpha, beta and gamma when concerning radiation. Astronomy, given it's advanced age, has acquired a frankly insane quantity of crap.

Finally of course there is π. There will be no π in this course.

In the course, that sat in this lockdown I am writing, we will focus on what I believe to be good practice. I intend for this to become a living document containing that which is important, not that which is prior.
